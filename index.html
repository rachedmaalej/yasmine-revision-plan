Notes & assumptions
  • Physics is the heaviest commitment in every plan — it is her weakest grade (C−, 71) and a core engineering subject, so it carries the largest share of hours.
  • French gets the least time — it is her strongest grade (A−, 93) and the lowest-priority subject for an engineering pathway. It is never dropped, just kept light.
  • Maths, Physics and Chemistry exams fall after 10 June (11, 12 and 15 June). Their main preparation happens inside this window; the days of 11–14 June are left as final-polish time, with Chemistry having the most runway.
  • No Biology or TOK exam appears on the timetable — Yasmine's sciences are Physics and Chemistry, and TOK is assessed by essay/exhibition, so neither is scheduled here.
  • School-day blocks assume she is home and settled by ~5:00 PM; times are suggestions — keep the subjects and order, shift the clock to fit her routine.
  • Every session should open with ~15 minutes of active recall of the previous session before new work begins.
