Plan A — Weighted Mastery

Study hours are allocated strictly by importance and difficulty. Physics carries the heaviest load — it is both the weakest grade and a core engineering subject — followed by Maths and Chemistry, then English and Psychology, with French kept lightest. Each subject moves cleanly from content review, through topic drills and past papers, into timed mocks and a final review timed against its exam. School evenings run two 2-hour blocks — 5:00–7:00 PM and 7:15–9:15 PM, four hours a day; weekends hold seven hours across three blocks. Week 2 bends around 29 May: the days before it concentrate on the Math Major and English Oral, and the evening after pivots gently back to the sciences.

Notes & assumptions
  • School evenings are now 4 hours — the first block is 5:00–7:00 PM (as requested), the second 7:15–9:15 PM. This trims one hour per school day from the original draft; if Yasmine wants the full 5 hours back, that hour can be added to the second block (ending 10:15 PM).
  • 29 May reshapes Week 2. Maths and English get the run-up days; English Oral prep is speaking practice — selecting extracts, refining delivery, timed mock orals — which is separate from the Literature essay work later on.
  • The 29 May evening is optional intensity. Four study hours are scheduled, but after two assessments in one day it is fine to cut it to a single 2-hour block and rest.
  • Excluded Physics topics: Waves & oscillations and Electricity & magnetism are off Yasmine's exam, so no revision time is allocated to them. The 6 hours freed by removing those three blocks were redistributed to Maths (+4 h, on 19 & 21 May) and Chemistry (+2 h, on 25 May) — Maths gets the larger share because those days fall in the run-up to the 29 May Math Major.
  • Physics is still the heaviest subject overall — weakest grade (C−) and core for engineering. French is the lightest — strongest grade (A−) and lowest engineering priority. It is never dropped, just kept light.
  • Maths, Physics and Chemistry IB exams fall after 10 June (11, 12 and 15 June). Their final polish happens 11–14 June, just outside this window — the evening of 11 June for Physics, and 12–14 June for Chemistry, which has the most runway.
  • No Biology or TOK exam appears on the timetable, so neither is scheduled. Clock times are suggestions — keep the subjects and order, shift the hours to fit her routine. Open every session with ~15 minutes of active recall of the previous one.
